About Me

The Translator Africa Needs

I grew up watching students, business owners, and policymakers make costly decisions about technology — not because they lacked intelligence, but because no one translated it for them. That gap cost businesses money. It cost students opportunities. It cost Africa time it cannot afford to lose.

So I became The Translator. I bridge Cameroon's National AI Strategy and the African Union's Continental AI Strategy to real-world implementation — ensuring ethical frameworks protect human dignity, data sovereignty, and cognitive freedom.

My work spans AI literacy education, responsible adoption consulting, and building locally-grounded AI systems designed for African contexts in education, agriculture, health, and governance.

"Africa doesn't have an AI problem. It has a translation problem."
